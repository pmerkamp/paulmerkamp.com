Don't Mind Me!

Ludum Dare 46

In April 2020, I participated in my first game jam, the 46th Ludum Dare. The theme was "Keep it alive", and the jam ran from April 17th to April 20th. I entered my game competitively, and it ended up ranking 354th among about 5,000 "compo" entries, putting it just outside top 7%.

The final version is playable in-browser on my itch.io.
The Unity project file, assets, and source code can also be found there.

Samuel

JCA Student Collaborative Project

In Spring 2020, I composed a 12-minute piece called "Samuel" for brass quartet and piano, to be premiered at Butler University's JCA Collaborative. This piece would have been played live, accompanied by dance choreographed by Jacob Shoup, one of Butler's fine dance majors and a good friend of mine. Unfortunately, due to the transition to an online semester, 2020's JCA Collaborative was cancelled, and the piece was never performed.

You can listen to a MIDI of the piece and view its score on YouTube here. (link broken)

Motivic Frequency and Development in
"Till Eulenspiegel's Merry Pranks"

Butler University Music Theory Exhibition, Fall 2018

Under the guidance of Butler University's Dr. Andrew Farina and Dr. Gail Lewis, I wrote a paper, designed a poster, and delivered a presentation all on the significance of motifs and their development in Richard Strauss' "Till Eulenspiegel's Merry Pranks". The poster was nominated by a panel of Butler faculty as one of the best shown at 2018's exhibition. It is viewable below.
